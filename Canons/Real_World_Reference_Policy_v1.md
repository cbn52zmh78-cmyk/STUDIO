# Real World Reference Policy v1.0

**Status:** Locked canon  
**Applies to:** MAGAZINE, editorial copy, narrative dialogue, History docs, campaign text, voiceover, captions  
**Principle:** The universe exists **inside** the real world — not sealed off from it.

---

## Core Rule

Our content operates in the same world the audience lives in. We **may and should** reference what we can legally reference:

- Public figures (name drops, comparisons, cultural context)
- Real places, institutions, festivals, publications
- Licensed materials we hold clearance for
- Historical facts and documented events
- Brands and works **by name** in editorial or dialogue when use is referential, not deceptive

The synthetic cast lives in a **real** cultural landscape. MAGAZINE covers them the way a real trade publication would — with real-world anchors.

---

## Three Reference Types

| Type | What it is | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Name / text reference** | Words only — no likeness, no logo | *"Awards-season energy reminiscent of Cannes '19"* |
| **Visual reference** | Logos, likeness, artwork, footage on screen | Celebrity face in a video; Nike swoosh on a shoe in frame |
| **Licensed use** | Cleared material in the final deliverable | Synced track; archival clip; brand partnership asset |

**Name drops are the lightest lift. Visual likeness and licensed use require more clearance.**

---

## GREEN — Proceed (Producer approves)

- **Public figure name drops** in editorial, dialogue, or voiceover — comparisons, homage, cultural commentary, opinion
- **Historical figures** in documentary / History context with factual framing
- **Real cities, venues, festivals, publications** as setting or context (*"debuted at Venice"*, *"Vogue-level lighting"*)
- **Nominative brand reference in text** — referring to a brand or work by name without implying endorsement (*"Chanel-tier silhouette"*, *"Scorsese pacing"*)
- **Licensed materials** with clearance logged in `Producers_Office/Release_Tracker/`

---

## YELLOW — Revise or log clearance before ship

- **Living public figure likeness** in AI-generated image/video (not just a name — their face/body/voice). Needs replica consent or counsel review per AB 2602 / NO FAKES trajectory
- **Deceased public figure likeness** for deceptive use — AB 1836 carveouts apply; documentary/commentary safer than fiction impersonation
- **Prominent trademark / logo in frame** — may need brand clearance for commercial distribution; text reference often fine where visual is not
- **Archival footage or news clips** — license or fair use opinion required
- **Music** in timed picture — sync + master, always

Log YELLOW items in `Release_Tracker` before `renders/approved/`.

---

## RED — Block

- **False defamatory statements of fact** about living persons (accusations presented as fact, not opinion)
- **Implied endorsement** — synthetic actor or real person appears to sponsor a product without deal
- **Uncleared licensed material** in a commercial deliverable passed off as cleared
- **Non-consensual deepfake** of a real person's likeness in intimate or deceptive context
- **Trademark / logo use** designed to confuse source or trade on goodwill without clearance (commercial ship)

---

## How MAGAZINE Uses the Real World

MAGAZINE is allowed — encouraged — to sound like it lives on newsstands next to real publications:

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| *"The Plantagenet epic drew comparisons to early Thrones pacing"* | *"Official HBO collaboration"* (without deal) |
| *"Shot on location energy — think Marfa, not matte painting"* | Generate Timothée Chalamet's face without clearance |
| *"Licensed cue: [track name]"* (with sync on file) | Drop a Billboard hit in final cut with no license |
| *"Eleanor of Aquitaine's documented 1173 rebellion"* | Fabricate a living journalist's quote as real |

**Name drop freely in copy. Generate likenesses of living people only with clearance.**
