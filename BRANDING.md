## 1. Entity Structure

| Entity | Role | Public-facing? |
|--------|------|---------------|
| **Upon Tyne Productions** | Production company. Legal entity. Owner of all IP. | Yes — credits, contracts, distribution |
| **STUDIO** | Production infrastructure. The system, pipeline, and operator. | Internal + select client-facing |
| **MAGAZINE** | In-universe editorial publication. Content brand. | Yes — audience-facing |

**Rule:** Upon Tyne Productions signs contracts and takes credits. STUDIO is how the work gets made. MAGAZINE is what audiences see.

## 2. Upon Tyne Productions

**Positioning:** Independent cinematic production company specializing in AI-assisted narrative, editorial, and historical visual work.

**Tagline options (Director to select):**

| Option | Tone |
|--------|------|
| *"Directed. Not generated."* | Asserts craft and authorial control over the AI-generated medium |
| *"Cinema at the speed of thought."* | Positions speed as a feature without sacrificing quality |
| *"Frame by frame. Character by character."* | Emphasizes granular craft and character work |
| *"The studio that ships."* | Operational — speaks to client confidence |

**Positioning statement (full):**  
Upon Tyne Productions is a cinematically-driven AI video production company. We use AI tools the way a DP uses a camera — as a precise instrument, not a gimmick. Every frame passes a legal gate. Every performer has an actor profile. Every scene earns its shot list. The result: visual work that looks like it came from a post-house, not a prompt window.

**Voice:** Assured. Precise. Never apologetic about using AI, never over-explaining it. The craft sells itself.

## 3. STUDIO — Internal Brand

STUDIO is the production system, not the audience-facing product. Its brand is operational:

**Identity:** STUDIO is where the production happens. Its visual language is utilitarian precision — the kind you see in a real production office: call sheets, legal verdicts, slate boards, compliance reports.

**Internal tone:** Producer's voice. Structured. Chain of command respected. "Legal RED is a hard no" is a brand statement.

**Filing discipline = brand discipline.** Every document named right, every gate logged, every slip recorded — that *is* the STUDIO brand. Sloppiness is off-brand by definition.

**File naming pattern:**
```
{Project}_{ContentType}_{YYYYMMDD}_{Version}.{ext}
Valentina_Rossi_Sculptural_Black_Coat_Editorial_20260616_1119_Shot_01.txt
Henry_II_Council_Scene_v1_20260616.json
```

## 4. MAGAZINE — Publishing Output

MAGAZINE is the publishing subfolder/format for Upon Tyne Productions' entertainment content — films, editorials, videos, and creative work produced from the full actor roster. It is not a separate brand; it is where the work ships.

**What it houses:** Cinematic output, editorial images and videos, short films, and any distributable creative content produced by the studio. Backed by a roster of ~100 original synthetic actors (men, women, transgender) — enough variety to cast real narrative work with genuine range.

**The ambition:** Original movies and cinematic content featuring a consistent, recurring cast of characters — not just one-off clips or historical renders. Real narrative filmmaking, with the studio's own actors, published in MAGAZINE format (print-ready, web, video, whatever the content calls for).

**Distribution options (TBD):** Online, social, direct download, film festivals (per `Distribution/`). Format is flexible — the content and cast are what's real.

**No tagline yet.** To be developed when publishing direction crystallizes.

## 5. Visual Direction (Reference, Not Locked)

These are directional. A full visual identity requires a designer pass.

**Color language:**
- **Upon Tyne Productions:** Near-black (#0D0D0D), warm white (#F5F0E8), gold accent (#C9A84C) — formal, credentialed, production company weight
- **STUDIO (internal):** Neutral gray system (#1A1A1A, #2E2E2E, #F0F0F0) — utilitarian, pipeline-grade
- **MAGAZINE:** Rich editorial — deep burgundy (#4A0E1F), cream (#FAF6EE), editorial black (#111111) — high-fashion print energy

**Typography direction:**
- Upon Tyne: Serif — Garamond, EB Garamond, or Cormorant Garamond (gravitas, permanence)
- MAGAZINE: Extended serif for headers (Canela, Freight Display, or Playfair Display XL) — fashion editorial
- STUDIO internal: Monospace for tool output and logs; clean sans-serif (Inter, DM Sans) for producer docs

**Logo direction (Upon Tyne):**  
Wordmark only. No icon initially. Letterpress aesthetic — the kind of thing that would appear embossed on a production slate or in the bottom-right corner of a call sheet.

## 6. Naming the Talent

Performer stage names carry brand weight. Rules:

- Stage name = the public-facing identity (Valentina Rossi, Yuna/Wendy, Blonde, etc.)
- Full names (Yuna Nakamura) appear in actor profiles and legal documents only
- MAGAZINE uses stage names in all copy
- Client deliverables use stage names unless contract specifies otherwise

When STUDIO capacity is offered through Stonebridge for client work (branding, commercials, logos):

**What we lead with:**
- Director control (not just generation)
- Legal compliance built in (not bolted on)
- Named performer consistency (not random faces)
- Deliverable-grade output (not experiment screenshots)

**What we never say to clients:**
- "AI-generated" as the headline (the result is, the process doesn't need to lead)
- "Prompt-based" 
- "Experimental"
