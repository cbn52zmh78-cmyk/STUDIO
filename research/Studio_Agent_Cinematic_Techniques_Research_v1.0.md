## 1. Cinematic Techniques & Directorial Styles

### Wes Anderson
**Signature Techniques**
- Planimetric composition + perfect symmetry
- Dollhouse framing / frontal staging
- Controlled, vibrant color palettes
- Meticulous production design
- Deadpan performance + slow, mechanical camera

**Prompting Language**  
"Planimetric symmetrical composition with perfect frontal staging and horizontal layering, Wes Anderson-style dollhouse framing, vibrant controlled pastel palette, deadpan performance."

### Sean Baker
**Signature Techniques**
- Naturalistic, observational handheld or minimal-crew style
- One-take or long-take energy
- Gritty yet empathetic framing of real locations and non-professional actors

**Prompting Language**  
"Naturalistic handheld camera following her through the crowded street, Sean Baker-style observational realism, shallow depth of field on her face while background remains chaotic and alive."

### Giallo & Brian De Palma Influence
**Signature Techniques**
- Stylized, operatic violence
- Expressionistic color and lighting
- Subjective/voyeuristic camera, bold tracking, dramatic zooms

**Prompting Language**  
"Giallo-inspired stylized murder sequence with bold tracking shot and lurid red lighting, De Palma-style subjective camera and dramatic zoom on the gloved hand."

### Sofia Coppola
**Signature Techniques**
- Negative space & isolation in wide compositions
- Soft, pastel, atmospheric lighting
- Lingering sensory texture and slow, fluid camera

**Prompting Language**  
"Sofia Coppola-style wide composition with generous negative space isolating her in the ornate room, soft pastel lighting and lens flare, slow drifting camera lingering on fabric texture and her distant expression."

### Additional Techniques You Use
- One-take shots
- Free-cam movement
- Impasto texture
- J-cuts / L-cuts

## 2. Visual Storytelling for Short-Form Cinematic Work

- Vignette or episodic structure linked by theme/motif
- Emotional arc in limited runtime: quick setup → rising internal tension → quiet revelation
- Use negative space, motivated camera, and color to create atmospheric impact

**Actionable Prompt Tip**  
Structure short clips as “emotional vignettes”:  
“Static wide shot establishing isolation, slow push-in revealing micro-expression, final lingering close-up with negative space.”

## 3. Historical / Period Accuracy for 3D & Visual Work (Medieval / Plantagenet / Norman)

**Key Elements**
- Clothing: layered wool/linen tunics, surcoats, wimples, chainmail
- Architecture: rounded Norman arches → pointed Gothic
- Props/Environments: rushes on floors, hand-forged tools, candlelight

**Reliable Sources**
- British Library digitized medieval manuscripts
- Museum of London / British Museum collections
- Academic reconstructions (Regia Anglorum, Medieval Combat Society)

**Prompting Language**  
"Accurate 12th-century Plantagenet noblewoman in layered wool surcoat and wimple, standing in stone castle with rounded Norman arches, natural candlelight and rushes on floor, historically precise textures."

## 4. AI Video / 3D Rendering Best Practices

- **Consistency**: Repeat key descriptors (clothing, lighting mood, palette, style reference) in every major beat.
- **Lighting**: Motivated practicals + layered planes.
- **Camera**: Prefer motivated movement; specify speed and purpose. Use rack focus, depth of field, and foreground/midground/background layering.

**Prompting Language**  
"Consistent character appearance across shots; motivated slow dolly in with shallow depth of field isolating her face while foreground rain streaks the window and background lights blur into bokeh."
