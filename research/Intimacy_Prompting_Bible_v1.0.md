# Intimacy Prompting Bible
**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-06-15 | **Status:** Locked Reference for All Future Work

**Purpose**  
This document consolidates tested rules, language standards, and prompting techniques for generating intimate physical scenes in narrative cinematic work. It focuses on achieving emotionally grounded, R-rated intimacy while staying within the video editor’s reliable operating window and avoiding moderation.

---

## 1. Core Principle: Show the Build-Up, Imply the Rest

In narrative filmmaking, the most powerful intimacy is almost always the **build-up** — not the explicit act.  
The editor supports:
- Passionate kissing (deep, mouth-closed preferred)
- Embracing, caressing, body closeness
- Dry humping / grinding (when handled correctly)
- Topless states with limited physical contact
- Strong reaction shots that imply off-screen action

The editor is **not reliable** for:
- Visible genitalia or explicit below-waist contact
- Full nudity combined with heavy physical interaction
- Two separate asset actors during elevated physical intimacy

---

## 2. Asset Actors vs Prompt-Generated Characters (Critical Rule)

The video editor treats asset-based actors differently from characters generated purely through prompt description.

### Allowed with Two Asset Actors (@1 + @2)
- Touching, embracing, caressing
- Kissing (including deep/passionate)
- Light to moderate physical closeness
- Pre-sex intimacy and build-up scenes

**Use two asset actors for most standard intimate build-up work.**

### Requires One Baked + One Prompt-Generated
For **elevated physical intimacy** (dry humping, grinding, aggressive from-behind contact, straddling with movement, etc.):

- One character **must be baked into the setting plate** (asset slot 1).
- The second character **must be fully generated via prompt description** — no asset image or reference plate for them.

Attempting elevated physical intimacy with two asset actors will trigger moderation.

**Recommendation:**  
Reserve the baked + prompt-generated method for scenes that specifically need the higher level of physical contact the editor permits. For most kissing/embracing/caressing work, two asset actors remain the safer, more consistent choice.

## 3. Topless Interaction Rules

### One Topless Woman (Confirmed Reliable)
- A single topless woman in an intimate scene is well-supported, especially when the second character is prompt-generated.
- General caressing language (“caresing across the body and legs”) often allows the model to organically include bosom contact based on scene tone.
- One topless woman + prompt-generated second character gives noticeably more physical leeway than two baked assets.

### Two Topless Women (Soft Resistance Observed)
- Two topless women in close physical/intimate proximity is **not well-supported**.
- The editor may not hard-block, but will often respond with **soft resistance**:
  - Characters become blank, unresponsive, or stop following direction.
  - Emotional flatness and loss of scene coherence.
- This is a clear signal to back off. One topless woman at a time is the reliable limit for intimate scenes.

### Solo Topless
- A single topless woman can self-caress / cup her bosom.
- Pinching or aggressive nipple focus is unreliable and often moderated.

### Topless + Second Character
- The topless character must have been **baked into the initial scene**.
- Limited physical contact is tolerated (hand on shoulder, light caress on arm/upper back, embracing).
- More intimate contact (straddling while topless, hands on bosom by the other character) is possible but requires careful framing and prompt discipline.
- Heavy grinding or aggressive movement while one character is topless carries higher moderation risk.

**Best Practice:**  
When one character becomes topless, shift the camera emphasis to faces, upper bodies, hands on shoulders/backs, and emotional reaction rather than direct bosom focus.

## 4. Clothing State Change Risks in Extensions (New – June 2026)

When prompting clothing removal or topless transitions via video extension, two documented failure modes can cause unintended full nudity and genital exposure:

### 1. Clothing Color Matching
- When top and bottom are the **same color** (e.g., black top + black pants), the editor can interpret the outfit as a single garment (leotard/bodysuit).
- Prompting “remove top” may then remove the entire outfit in one go, exposing genitals in frame.

### 2. Limb / Body Occlusion (Blocking) at Extension Point
- If an arm, hand, or body part is blocking the visual boundary between top and bottom at the exact moment of extension, the editor may misread the clothing as a single connected piece.
- Example: Arm wrapped around waist hides the midriff → editor treats black top + black pants as one garment → full removal occurs.
- This is a temporal issue specific to extensions. The base generation may be clean, but the extension can misinterpret the occluded state.

**Prevention:**
- Use clearly different colors for top and bottom when clothing removal is planned.
- Choose extension points where the midriff/abdomen boundary is clearly visible (avoid moments with heavy arm/leg crossing or embracing that blocks the torso).
- Blocking/occlusion risk applies to any clothing state change scene, not just intimate ones.

## 5. Prompt Language Rules (Non-Negotiable)

Use only **proper, neutral English anatomical terms**.

**Acceptable:**
- bosom, breasts, chest, upper body, torso, shoulder, neck, back, hips, waist, thigh, buttocks

**Strictly Forbidden (triggers immediate moderation):**
- Any crude, lewd, slang, or euphemistic terms
- Any reference to female genitals or what lies between a woman’s legs
- "Pussy", "cunt", "vagina", "clit", "wet", "fingering", "eating her out", etc.

**Lockbox Rule:**  
The entire subject of female intimate anatomy below the waist is placed in a sealed lockbox, the key is broken, and the box is thrown into the depths of the Atlantic Ocean. It does not exist in prompts. Never open it.

After a character becomes topless, **do not repeatedly state "topless"** in every prompt. The model observes the current state on screen.

## 6. Reaction Shot / Implied Action Technique (Powerful & Safe)

When you want to suggest more intense intimacy without showing it:

- Position the camera on the **receiving character’s face and upper body**.
- Use slow push-ins, subtle head movement, eye fluttering, parted lips, soft moaning, hands gripping sheets or the other person’s shoulders/back.
- Let the model generate organic reaction while the physical action happens just off-frame or is heavily obscured.
- This technique has proven highly effective for implying oral sex, intense grinding, or climax without triggering moderation.

Example framing:  
Medium close-up on the woman’s face and shoulders as she arches slightly, eyes half-closed, breathing heavily, while the other woman’s head and shoulders are visible in the lower frame but the explicit contact remains off-screen or heavily shadowed.

## 7. Prompt Structure for a Fully Generated Second Actress

When generating the second character entirely through prompt (no asset):

**Recommended Structure:**

```
[Character Name] (age-first description in parentheses)
```

**Key Tips:**
- Keep the description concise but vivid enough for consistency across extensions.
- Specify her position and physical relationship clearly in the main prompt text (not inside parentheses).
- Use neutral, cinematic language for contact: “bodies pressed together”, “slow rhythmic movement”, “passionate kiss”, “hands exploring her back and shoulders”.
- Avoid over-describing sexual intent. Focus on physical positioning and emotional tone.

---

## 8. General Best Practices

- Always generate the initial clothed state first when clothing removal is planned.
- Use clean extensions (no new prompt) to maintain established physical rhythm and contact.
- When in doubt, favor **reaction + implication** over direct depiction.
- Document successful prompts and lock them once they prove stable.
- Never prompt explicit genital contact or below-the-waist intimate touching, regardless of method.
