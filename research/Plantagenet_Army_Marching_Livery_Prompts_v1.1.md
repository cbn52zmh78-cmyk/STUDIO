## 1. Historical Canon & Rules (Locked)

**Livery & Identification System**  
Lords issued retainers livery jackets/coats in household colors (often two main colors) plus personal or dynastic badge. Worn over armor (brigandine, jack, or plate) for instant recognition. Badges could be embroidered, painted, or metal. Banners and standards were the primary unit markers on the march.

**Elite Men-at-Arms Appearance**  
Transitional/full plate with high variation even within one retinue: mismatched pieces, repairs, personal engravings, different helmet styles (sallet, armet, kettle hat, bascinet). Wealthy retainers often wore colorful livery coats over steel.

**Marching Column Visuals**  
Dense, relatively orderly column on road or track. Elite troops clustered near lord's banner. Dust, banners streaming, mixed but cohesive livery colors, weapons (longbows, bills, poleaxes) visible. Commander and household knights near the front or centre of their division.
