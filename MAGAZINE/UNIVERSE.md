# MAGAZINE — In-Universe Showcase Canon

**Status:** Locked creative principle  
**Producer note:** The audience experiences performances. Origin is a production detail, not the product.

---

## What MAGAZINE Is

MAGAZINE is our trade face — the editorial layer that presents this studio's actors, films, campaigns, and moments **as if the universe already exists.**

Not "AI-generated content about fake people."  
**A living magazine covering a living world.**

If you get the video — the performance, the look, the story — the mind doesn't audit humanity. It responds. That's the product.

---

## Creative Rule: Exist, Don't Explain

| In-universe (always) | Out-of-universe (production/legal only) |
|----------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Cover lines, editorials, runway captions | Compliance reports, replica consents |
| "Valentina Rossi for Vogue-level editorial" | CARA rating targets, age policy |
| Film stills, BTS tone, campaign drops | E&O binders, platform disclosure when required |
| Actor profiles as careers | Tool logs, pipeline paths |

**On the page and on screen:** these people have careers. These films have releases. This magazine has issues.

---

## What "Real" Means Here

- **Performances are real** — emotion, camera, pacing, physics, continuity.
- **Editorial is real** — composition, lighting, styling, narrative intent.
- **The universe is real** — cross-reference actors across GFE, narrative, History, editorial without breaking the fourth wall in consumer-facing assets.
- **The world is real** — this universe sits inside ours. Name-drop public figures, reference Cannes, nod to Vogue, cite licensed material, anchor History to documented fact. See `Canons/Real_World_Reference_Policy_v1.md`.

What is synthetic is a **production method**, not a creative excuse for sloppy work — and not a reason to pretend culture outside the studio doesn't exist.

---

## Real World vs. In-Universe (both on)

| Layer | Example |
|-------|---------|
| **In-universe** | *"Valentina Rossi covers our March issue"* |
| **Real world** | *"Shot with Prestige drama lighting — think late-90s W magazine"* |
| **Licensed** | *Cue: cleared track from Release Tracker* |
| **Never mix wrongly** | Don't claim Valentina *endorsed* a real brand without a deal; don't generate a living star's face without clearance |

**Talk about the real world in copy. Clear likenesses and licenses before you put them on screen.**

---

## Producer Standards for MAGAZINE Work

1. **Casting plate before hero** — every model must look like the same person across shots.
2. **Numerical age in prompts** — compliance field; never breaks in-universe copy.
3. **No meta-AI language in editorial copy** — no "generated," "synthetic," "fake actor" in consumer-facing text.
4. **Cinema bar** — Vogue / runway / prestige trailer energy. Always.
5. **Cross-promote in-universe** — PI_Story stills, GFE moments, History figures can appear as magazine features when continuity holds.
6. **Real-world anchors** — public figures, places, festivals, licensed works by name when legally sound (`Canons/Real_World_Reference_Policy_v1.md`).
