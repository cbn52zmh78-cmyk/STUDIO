## Part 1: Historical Research

**Livery & Identification**
- Lords provided retainers with livery coats/jackets in their two primary colors + badge.
- Examples: murrey & blue (York), red/white rose, white boar, sun in splendour, etc.

**Armor Styles (Elite Men-at-Arms Focus)**
- Transitional to full plate: sallet helmets, brigandine, breastplate + placart, articulated limb harness, mail voiders at joints.
- High variation even in same retinue: mismatched pieces, repairs, personal engravings, different helmet styles (sallet, armet, kettle hat).

**Weapons**
- Longbows, bills, poleaxes, pollaxes, swords, rondel daggers, lances (dismounted).

**Formations & Appearance**
- Marching columns: dense, orderly with banners, dust, elite troops grouped around lord.
- Elite appearance: colorful livery coats over steel harness, mixed but high-quality armor.
