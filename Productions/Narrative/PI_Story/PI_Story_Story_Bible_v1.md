## Logline
A strung-out private investigator — once a cop, now an information broker — kills the hitman who murdered his
partner, then is quietly maneuvered by a chess-master police lieutenant into a one-man suicide run on the mob,
never knowing he was played. Everyone who could expose the truth dies; the lieutenant wins — until the loose
thread she forgot pulls.

## Genre / tone / influences
Dark noir crime thriller. **Tarantino × Scorsese × L.A. Confidential.** Parallel timelines, long takes,
false climax, circular ending. Grounded, ugly, human — not stylized action.

## Theme
**No one escapes their actions.** The circular ending (the CI's sedan returns) is the thesis.

## AUTHORIAL CANON (Benjamin's locked decisions — 2026-06-19)
1. **PI & Hitman share exactly ONE scene** (the bar convergence). The '82 class ring — the dead partner's —
   is the PI's in-scene CONFIRMATION that this is the man who killed his partner. Do NOT invent additional
   PI/Hitman scenes. (Craft requirement: establish the partner's death + the ring's existence earlier in the
   PI's world so the reveal carries weight — without a Hitman scene.)
2. **Femme Fatale = a pawn with ILLUSORY agency.** She believes she's playing the game; she is erratic and
   impulsive, and her bad decisions (the bathroom drowning) get her killed. Her tragedy is the GAP between
   how in-control she feels and how disposable she is — dramatic irony, not thwarted heroism. Do NOT write
   her as a competent schemer or a heroic victim.
3. **The CI handoff scene (Act I) runs LONGER** — give the CI presence and weight, so the Act III circular
   execution lands as a closed loop, not a twist out of nowhere.
4. **Violence is CLUMSY & DESPERATE, by design.** A drug-addicted, half-crazed PI vs. ordinary mobsters who
   are NOT professional fighters. Ugly, exhausting, scavenged, real. "Not every fight is John Wick." The
   Act III rampage must read as desperation, not choreography.

## Characters
- **The PI** — ex-cop turned info broker turned murderer. Driven by his partner's death; numbing himself
  with drink/drugs; dissociating after the kill. His dead partner is the "voice of reason" vision.
- **The Hitman** — calm, methodical professional; killed the PI's partner (wears the '82 ring). Valued mob
  associate; walks into the trap he doesn't see. One scene with the PI.
- **The Femme Fatale** — works the club; the Lieutenant's leveraged CI; believes she has control; impulsive
  → fatal. Loves(?) the PI; sets up the Hitman; drowns a woman in panic; killed by the Lieutenant.
- **The Lieutenant** — the hidden chess master. Loving mother/wife at home, cold manipulator at work. Directs
  the Femme Fatale, kills her personally, stages the body to push the PI at the mob, spins the press
  conference (L.A. Confidential). Believes she's won.
- **The Female Mob Boss** — runs the club/organization; fond of and trusting toward the Hitman; dies in the
  Act III mutual kill with the PI.
- **The CI** — the dark sedan / Act I package exchange; the circular device — executes the Lieutenant at the
  end. (Needs more Act I presence per canon #3.)
- **Minor:** Bar Owner (wants assurance, plastic sheeting), eager young Beat Cop, two SUV crew members,
  Mob Boss's sons.

## Structure (3 acts — non-linear/parallel)
- **Act I** — parallel timelines (PI long-take block / Hitman fragmented block) converge at the empty bar;
  ends on the FALSE CLIMAX (PI tortures/torches the Hitman). 16 beats.
- **Act II** — non-linear open (Lieutenant ran the Femme Fatale all along); PI's Fear-and-Loathing spiral;
  Lieutenant kills the Femme Fatale + stages the body → catalyst that drives the PI at the mob.
- **Act III** — clumsy/desperate suicide run at the country club; mutual kill with the Mob Boss; final
  cigarette + sunset; Lieutenant's press-conference false victory; CI's circular execution. Fade to black.
