**HOW MANDARIN QUESTIONS WORK**
Mandarin does NOT flip word order to make a question (unlike English).
- English: "Where IS he?" (verb moved forward)
- Mandarin: "Tā zài nǎr?" (他在哪儿？) — literally "He at where?" — same order, question word at the end

**KEY QUESTION SENTENCES**
- "Who is that?" → Nà shì shéi? (那是谁？)
- "What is this?" → Zhè shì shénme? (这是什么？)
- "Where is [place]?" → [Place] zài nǎr? ([地方]在哪儿？)
- "Why?" → Wèishéme? (为什么？)
- "How do I get there?" → Zěnme qù? (怎么去？)
- "When?" → Shénme shíhou? (什么时候？)
- "How many do you want?" → Nǐ yào jǐ ge? (你要几个？)

**MEASURE WORDS — INTRODUCTION**
In Mandarin, you can't say "three books" directly — you need a measure word: "three [measure] books."
The general measure word is gè (个). For now, use gè for everything.
- "Three of these" → zhè sān gè (这三个)
- "Two people" → liǎng gè rén (两个人) — note: liǎng (两) = "two" when counting with measure words
