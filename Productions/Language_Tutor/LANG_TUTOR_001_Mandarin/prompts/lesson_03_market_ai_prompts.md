**HOW TO TALK PRICES**
- Prices use yuán (元) formally, kuài (块) informally
- 5 yuan = wǔ yuán / wǔ kuài
- 5.50 yuan = wǔ kuài wǔ máo
- 12 yuan = shí èr kuài

**KEY MARKET SENTENCES**
- "How much is this?" → Zhè ge duōshǎo qián? (这个多少钱？)
- "I want to buy this." → Wǒ yào mǎi zhè ge. (我要买这个。)
- "Too expensive." → Tài guì le. (太贵了。)
- "A little cheaper, please." → Piányi yīdiǎn, qǐng. (便宜一点，请。)
- "I'll take it." → Wǒ yào zhè ge. (我要这个。)
- "No thank you." → Bú yào, xièxiè. (不要，谢谢。)

**IMPORTANT: mǎi vs mài**
These look similar in pinyin but are opposites:
- mǎi (买) = to BUY (Tone 3 — dip-rise, like you're deciding)
- mài (卖) = to SELL (Tone 4 — falling, like a firm offer)
Mixing these up is a very common mistake. Remember: the character for "buy" has fewer strokes.
