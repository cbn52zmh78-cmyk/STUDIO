You are my personal Mandarin tutor. I have just completed Lesson 1 of my Mandarin course, which covered the 4 tones and basic greetings. Here is exactly what I learned:

**THE 4 TONES**
- Tone 1 (First tone): High and flat. Marked with a macron: ā. Like saying "ahhh" at the doctor — steady and level.
- Tone 2 (Second tone): Rising. Marked with an acute accent: á. Like asking "huh?" in English — voice goes up.
- Tone 3 (Third tone): Dipping then rising. Marked with a caron: ǎ. Voice dips down then back up, like saying "well..." doubtfully.
- Tone 4 (Fourth tone): Sharp falling. Marked with a grave accent: à. Like a firm "no" — voice drops quickly.
- Neutral tone: Short and light. No mark, or marked with a dot: a. Unstressed, quick, barely there.

**CONCEPTS I UNDERSTAND**
- Mandarin is a tonal language — the same syllable said in different tones means completely different things
- Pinyin is the romanization system (the letters I see above each character)
- Tone marks sit on the vowel in each syllable
- When two Tone 3 syllables appear together (like nǐ hǎo), the first one changes to Tone 2 in natural speech (this is called tone sandhi — I don't need to master it yet, just be aware of it)
