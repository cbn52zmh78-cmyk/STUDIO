## THE MOST IMPORTANT RULE: WORD ORDER STAYS THE SAME

In English, we flip the verb to ask a question:
- "He IS here." → "WHERE is he?"

In Mandarin, **nothing moves**. The question word goes exactly where the answer would go:
- "Tā zài nǎr?" (他在哪儿？) — literally "He at where?" ✓
- NOT "Zài tā nǎr?" ✗

This actually makes Mandarin questions easier to form once you accept the rule.

## MEASURE WORDS — INTRODUCTION

In Mandarin, you can't put a number directly before a noun. You need a **measure word** in between.

**English:** "three books"  
**Mandarin:** "three [measure] books" → sān běn shū (三本书)

For now, use **gè (个)** as your all-purpose measure word — it works for most things and people will understand you:
- "Three of these" → zhè sān gè (这三个)
- "One person" → yī gè rén (一个人)
- "How many do you want?" → Nǐ yào jǐ gè? (你要几个？)

**Special rule:** When "two" comes before a measure word, use **liǎng (两)** instead of èr (二):
- Two people → liǎng gè rén (两个人) ✓  
- NOT èr gè rén ✗  
(This same rule applies to time: 2 o'clock = liǎng diǎn, not èr diǎn — from Lesson 2!)
