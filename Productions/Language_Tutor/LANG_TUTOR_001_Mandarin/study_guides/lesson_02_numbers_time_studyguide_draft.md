## BUILDING BIGGER NUMBERS

The pattern: **[tens digit] + shí (十) + [ones digit]**

| Number | Mandarin | Breakdown |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| 11 | shí yī (十一) | ten + one |
| 15 | shí wǔ (十五) | ten + five |
| 20 | èr shí (二十) | two + ten |
| 21 | èr shí yī (二十一) | two + ten + one |
| 35 | sān shí wǔ (三十五) | three + ten + five |
| 50 | wǔ shí (五十) | five + ten |
| 99 | jiǔ shí jiǔ (九十九) | nine + ten + nine |
| 100 | yī bǎi (一百) | one + hundred |

**Quick tip:** For 10–19, you can drop the initial yī. 10 = shí (not yī shí). 11 = shí yī. For 20 and above, you always need the tens digit: èr shí (twenty), not just shí shí.

## HOW TO SAY THE TIME

**Pattern:** [AM/PM] + [hour] + diǎn + [minutes] + fēn

| English | Mandarin |
|---------|----------|
| 3:00 | sān diǎn (三点) |
| 3:30 | sān diǎn bàn (三点半) |
| 3:15 | sān diǎn shí wǔ fēn (三点十五分) |
| 8:00 AM | shàngwǔ bā diǎn (上午八点) |
| 2:45 PM | xiàwǔ liǎng diǎn sì shí wǔ fēn (下午两点四十五分) |
| 9:00 PM | wǎnshàng jiǔ diǎn (晚上九点) |

**Note:** For 2 o'clock, use **liǎng diǎn** (两点) not èr diǎn. In Mandarin, liǎng (两) replaces èr (二) when talking about "two of something" — two o'clock, two people, two items.
