## ⚠ CRITICAL: mǎi vs mài

These look almost identical in pinyin but mean opposite things. This is one of the most common beginner mistakes.

| Pinyin | Character | Meaning | Tone | Memory trick |
|--------|-----------|---------|------|-------------|
| mǎi | 买 | to **BUY** | 3rd (dip-rise — like deciding) | The character has FEWER strokes — you give money away |
| mài | 卖 | to **SELL** | 4th (falling — like a firm offer) | The character has MORE strokes — you receive money in |

Practice: say them back to back out loud — mǎi (buy) · mài (sell) · mǎi · mài.

## HOW PRICES WORK

Prices use kuài (块) informally (spoken Mandarin) and yuán (元) formally (written/official):

| Price | Mandarin | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| ¥5 | wǔ kuài (五块) | simple |
| ¥5.50 | wǔ kuài wǔ máo (五块五毛) | kuài then máo |
| ¥12 | shí èr kuài (十二块) | use numbers from Lesson 2 |
| ¥0.80 | bā máo (八毛) | máo only — no kuài needed |
| ¥100 | yī bǎi kuài (一百块) | |

## A COMPLETE SHOPPING EXCHANGE

| Who | Mandarin | English |
|-----|----------|---------|
| You | Zhè ge duōshǎo qián? (这个多少钱？) | How much is this one? |
| Seller | Shí kuài. (十块。) | Ten yuan. |
| You | Tài guì le. Piányi yīdiǎn? (太贵了。便宜一点？) | Too expensive. A little cheaper? |
| Seller | Hǎo de. Qī kuài. (好的。七块。) | Okay. Seven yuan. |
| You | Hǎo de. Wǒ yào zhè ge. (好的。我要这个。) | Okay. I'll take this one. |
| Seller | Gěi nǐ. (给你。) | Here you go. |
| You | Xièxiè! (谢谢！) | Thank you! |
