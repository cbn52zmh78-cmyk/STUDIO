SPECIAL STATUS: UNDECIPHERED — This is the Archive's honest episode about the limits of corpus work.
The script can be provisionally vocalised using borrowed Linear B sign values. The language it records is not identified. No scholarly consensus exists on decipherment.

CORPUS ANCHOR
- ~1,500 documents: clay tablets (administrative / accounting), stone libation tables, inscribed vessels and seals; c. 1800–1450 BCE; Crete (Akrotiri, Haghia Triada, Knossos, Malia, Phaistos, Zakros) and Aegean islands
- Held at: Heraklion Archaeological Museum, Crete; Ashmolean Museum, Oxford; National Archaeological Museum, Athens
- GORILA (Recueil des inscriptions en Linéaire A): definitive corpus, L. Godart & J.-P. Olivier, EFRE, 1976–1985
- Haghia Triada tablets: largest single archive (~150 tablets); accounting format
- Libation formula: recurring sequence on stone offering tables — most-studied text unit

LINEAR A / LINEAR B RELATIONSHIP
- Linear B (Mycenaean Greek) deciphered 1952 by Michael Ventris (architect, amateur linguist)
- Linear A and Linear B share ~80% of sign forms
- Applying Linear B phonetic values to Linear A produces sequences in no known language
- This confirms Linear A was not used for Greek; underlying language unidentified (Minoan)

DEMONSTRATION — LIBATION FORMULA
i-da-ma-te (provisional reading using Linear B values; sign sequence: 𐘂-𐘳-𐘼-𐘉)
Nature: PROVISIONAL phonetics from Linear B parallels; meaning and linguistic affiliation UNKNOWN
Recurs on stone libation tables at Apodoulou, Haghia Triada, Knossos, Kato Zakros, Malia, Poros, Psychro, etc.
May be: theonym, toponym, ritual phrase, or dedicatee name — all hypotheses, none established

ATTESTATION STATUS
Script: Identifiable sign inventory (~56 syllabic signs + logographic/ideographic signs) — reading PROVISIONAL
Language: Minoan — UNKNOWN family; not Indo-European, not Semitic, not Hurrian (all proposed and rejected or unconfirmed)
Decipherment status: None accepted by scholarly consensus as of 2025
