Title: The Language That Rewrote the Family Tree
Language: Hittite (Classical Old Hittite / Middle Hittite)
Target: 69 seconds · PG · documentary-host · David-001

CORPUS ANCHOR
- ~30,000 clay tablets excavated at Hattusa (Boğazkale, Turkey), 1906–present
- Held at: Istanbul Archaeological Museums; Museum of Anatolian Civilizations, Ankara; Berlin
- Primary texts: Treaty of Kadesh (Egyptian-Hittite, c. 1259 BCE); Anitta text (oldest Hittite, 18th c. BCE); CTH (Catalogue of Hittite Texts) cuneiform tablets
- Bilingual: many administrative texts in Hittite + Akkadian; hieroglyphic Luwian companion inscriptions
- Publication standard: Keilschrifturkunden aus Boghazköi (KBo), Keilschrifttexte aus Boghazköi (KUB)

DEMONSTRATION LINE
nu GIM-an dUTU-uš nepišaz andan uizzi
Transliteration from KBo 17.74 (Hittite ritual prayer)
Gloss: "and/now as the Sun god comes in from heaven"
Nature: ATTESTED cuneiform text; phonemic realisation RECONSTRUCTED where orthography under-specifies

ATTESTATION STATUS
Attested: Yes (cuneiform syllabary directly records C+V sequences)
Reconstructed: Phonemic contrasts masked by cuneiform (e.g., voiced/voiceless stops); accent

HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE
- First Indo-European language attested in written form
- Confirmed Ferdinand de Saussure's laryngeal hypothesis (1879): Hittite preserved laryngeals written as ḫ
- Forced wholesale revision of the PIE phonological model (laryngeal theory)
- Treaty of Kadesh: earliest surviving international peace treaty; both Hittite and Egyptian versions preserved

GATE 0 NOTES
- All corpus material ≥2,700 years old; public domain
- No living community claims Hittite as heritage language; no cultural sensitivity concerns
- Laryngeal theory explanation kept at accessible level; no contested political claims
- Historical context (Hittite Empire, Anatolia, Battle of Kadesh) is widely documented in peer-reviewed scholarship
