- Coptic Orthodox liturgical texts: Bohairic dialect; living tradition; Diocese of the Coptic Orthodox Church worldwide
- Ebers Papyrus (Demotic phase, Egyptian): medical texts in Late Egyptian/Demotic; precursor writing tradition

CHAMPOLLION CONNECTION
Coptic provided Champollion the vowel anchor for decoding Demotic and hieroglyphic Egyptian.
1822: Jean-François Champollion used knowledge of Coptic to phonetically map hieroglyphic signs on the Rosetta Stone — because Coptic revealed what vowels Egyptian words carried.

DIALECT MAP
- Sahidic (southern / Upper Egypt): 3rd–11th c. CE; literary standard; Nag Hammadi corpus
- Bohairic (northern / Nile Delta): 9th c. CE–present; current liturgical standard of Coptic Orthodox Church
- Fayyumic, Akhmimic, Lycopolitan, Sub-Akhmimic: regional dialects, mostly extinct; important for comparative phonology
