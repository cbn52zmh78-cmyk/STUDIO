## The product

A complete Mandarin learning package: video lesson + companion PDF study guide + AI tutor prompt pack. Each component is useful alone; together they form a self-contained learning system that goes far beyond a video series.

**The hook the market doesn't have yet:** the AI prompt component turns any ChatGPT/Claude project into a personalized Mandarin tutor that already knows exactly what lesson the student is on, what vocabulary they covered, and what to practice next. Watch the video, follow the PDF, then open your AI and keep going — it's already set up.

---

## Product architecture

### Three components per lesson:

**1. The Video (~3 min)**  
Synthetic host (Chinese woman, late 20s) teaches the lesson on camera — warm, clear, direct. Not a lecture: she speaks to the viewer, demonstrates pronunciation, models conversation. The face of the product.

**2. The Companion PDF**  
Follows along with the video beat by beat. Designed to print or keep open on a second screen.  
Contents per lesson:
- Lesson header: title, episode number, "what you'll know by the end"
- Vocabulary table: Character · Pinyin (with tone marks) · English · Example sentence
- Tone/pronunciation guide (visual for Lesson 1; reference card for subsequent eps)
- Key phrases block (pull-quotes of the most important lines from the video)
- Practice section: fill-in, matching, or write-out exercises (the student does these alongside or after the video)
- "Before next lesson" note: one or two things to practice before Ep 2
- Quick-reference footer: all vocab from this lesson in one compact table

**3. The AI Prompt Pack**  
A ready-to-paste system prompt (and a few conversation starters) that sets up a Claude/ChatGPT/Gemini project as a personalized Mandarin tutor calibrated to this exact lesson. The student pastes it once into their AI of choice and they have a tutor who:
- Knows what vocabulary they covered (it's in the prompt)
- Knows which tones/concepts were introduced
- Can quiz them, run conversations, correct their attempts, give examples
- Advances at the right level (not too easy, not skipping ahead)

Format: one main system prompt + 3–5 "first message" starters (e.g. "Quiz me on the 4 tones", "Let's practice greetings", "Explain the neutral tone again")  
Compatibility: written to work across Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, and Copilot — no lock-in  
Delivered as: a text file OR embedded in the PDF as a clearly boxed "Copy & Paste" section

## Scalability — the factory angle

This is not a one-language product. The components all template cleanly:

- **Swap the language** → new video series + new PDFs + new prompts. Same structure, same host type, different content.
- **Swap the subject** → Math, Coding, Music Theory, History — any topic that fits a "5-lesson intro arc" works in this format.
- **Swap the level** → Beginner Mandarin pack → Intermediate Mandarin pack → Business Mandarin pack. Stack-able purchases.

The AI prompt pack is actually the highest-margin component (pure text, zero marginal cost) and the hardest for competitors to replicate well — it requires real subject knowledge to write a prompt that actually teaches correctly. That's the moat.

## Curriculum outline — Mandarin Level 1 (5-lesson arc)

| Ep | Title | Video content | PDF focus | AI Prompt specialization |
|----|-------|--------------|-----------|--------------------------|
| 1 | "Hello, Mandarin — The 4 Tones" | The 4 tones + neutral; nǐ hǎo + 5 basic greetings | Tone chart, IPA basics, greeting vocab table | Quiz tones, say-back corrections, minimal-pair drills |
| 2 | "Numbers and Time" | 1–100 counting; time-of-day phrases | Number grid, time vocab, clock visual | Number quizzing, time Q&A, "what time is it?" practice |
| 3 | "At the Market" | Shopping: prices, quantities, please/thank you, bargaining politely | Market vocab table, price-sentence templates | Role-play: student buys something, host corrects |
| 4 | "Who, What, Where" | Question words: shéi, shénme, nǎr, wèishéme, zěnme | Question-word reference card, sentence-build exercises | Q&A chains, "ask me 10 questions" mode |
| 5 | "Your First Conversation" | Full 5-exchange dialogue; repair strategies ("say that again?"); putting it together | Full dialogue transcript + annotated version, repair phrases | Full conversation mode, error correction, "let's talk" |

All scripts must include:
- Pinyin WITH tone marks throughout (màn, not man)
- Character alongside pinyin at first introduction per word
- Tonal emphasis flagged in shot notes for the host
- Authenticity gate: cousin reviews every script before production

## Format spec

**Duration target:** ~3 min content + 5s provenance card ≈ ~185s total  
**Shot count:** ~20–24 shots (8–9s avg)  
**Structure template per episode:**

```
01_cold_open      (7–8s)  host greeting + episode number/title card
02_hook           (7–8s)  "by the end of this you'll be able to..."
03–20_lesson      (8–9s × ~18 shots) core content
    pattern per vocab block: INTRODUCE → MODEL (slow) → MODEL (natural speed) → PRACTICE CUE
    tonal emphasis shots: flag slow_pronunciation:true in shot note
21_review         (8s)    quick recap — 3 key things from this lesson
22_preview        (7s)    teaser for next ep
23_close          (7s)    warm close + "practice today" encouragement
24_provenance     (5s)    "Synthetic host · AI-generated · Educational · 
                          Not a certified instructor · Upon Tyne Productions"
```

**Audio:**
- Mandarin terms: speech_text must include pinyin WITH tone marks
- No music under lesson core (distraction)
- Soft neutral bed under intro/close only (brighter than BED-WARM-001; research a "light study / morning focus" feel)
