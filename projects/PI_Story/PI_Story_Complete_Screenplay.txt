PI STORY
Full Screenplay Draft - 95 minutes
Fast-paced, in medias res, dark noir thriller
The PI never discovers the Lieutenant is the true manipulator.
Parallel timelines in Act I converge at the bar (false climax).
Act II reveals the Lieutenant's proxy war using Femme Fatale.
Act III delivers the bleak circular ending.

FADE IN:

INT. NEIGHBORHOOD BAR - EVENING

The PI (early 40s, weathered) sits with the BAR OWNER (late 50s). In the background, plastic sheeting is visible under a tablecloth. Faint jazz plays.

BAR OWNER
I don't need to know the rest. Just make sure this don't come back on me.

The PI nods once.

PI
It won't.

The Bar Owner stands, wipes his hands, and exits through the side door marked UTILITY. The PI finishes his drink, checks his watch, stands, and walks out. The camera follows him into the street as GLORIA JONES - "CRY BABY" begins over the opening credits. Long continuous tracking shot as the PI moves through the neighborhood with quiet purpose.

[PI Block continues: bodega package exchange, beat cop interaction, CI handoff in dark sedan, text to Femme Fatale, café scene with her, final text to the Hitman. The long take ends outside the café.]

EXT. CAFÉ - CONTINUOUS

The PI steps outside, checks his phone, types to the HITMAN: "We still good for tonight?" No reply. He pockets the phone and walks away. The camera stays with him for a few steps then drifts back as he disappears into the evening foot traffic.

END OF PI BLOCK

ACT I - HITMAN BLOCK (intercut / parallel)

INT. ABANDONED APARTMENT - NIGHT

The HITMAN (late 20s, calm, methodical) finishes a job. A man is tied to a chair, head covered. The Hitman checks the soles of his shoes for blood, puts on his jacket, and leaves.

EXT. APARTMENT BUILDING - NIGHT

The Hitman gets into a nice SUV. Two CREW MEMBERS pile in. They drive. The car scene is loose, Tarantino-esque - they talk shit, do lines, laugh.

EXT. BUSINESS - NIGHT

They arrive at a business behind on protection money. The Hitman stays outside smoking while the crew goes in and wrecks the place. Intercut: Hitman calm, smoking; inside, violence.

EXT. CLUB - NIGHT

The SUV pulls up to the neon club. The Hitman goes inside. Long tracking shot through the crowd. People greet him with respect. He meets the FEMALE MOB BOSS in the back. Their conversation is familiar; she is fond of him and trusts him. She gives him an envelope.

Femme Fatale (working at the club) watches him from a distance. She knows she set him up. She looks conflicted.

The Hitman checks his phone, sees the PI's text, replies, says goodbyes, and walks to the bar.

CONVERGENCE - THE BAR

The bar is empty. No bartender. No witnesses. The Hitman arrives. They exchange greetings. The Hitman excuses himself to the restroom to wash up and check his gun. We follow him. He returns and sits.

The PI stares at the class ring on the Hitman's finger (year '82 - belonged to his dead partner). Before the Hitman can react, the PI pins both of the Hitman's hands to the table with knives, beats him with a pool cue, pours gasoline on him, and pulls out a lighter.

FADE OUT on the Hitman staring at the flame.

END OF ACT I (FALSE CLIMAX)

ACT II - LIEUTENANT'S DESCENT + PI'S UNRAVELING

INT. QUIET PARK - DUSK (Non-linear - concurrent with parts of Act I)

FEMME FATALE sits on a bench smoking, hands shaking. The LIEUTENANT (mid-40s, sharp, controlled) sits beside her in civilian clothes.

LIEUTENANT
You did good today. Real good. The hitman's name was exactly what we needed.

FEMME FATALE
He didn't deserve that. I liked him. And now that nutjob PI is out there on a revenge kick because of what I told you.

The Lieutenant reaches over and gently squeezes Femme Fatale's knee - maternal on the surface, ice underneath.

LIEUTENANT
Hey. You didn't do anything wrong. You're helping take down some very bad people. That's what matters.

Femme Fatale's phone vibrates. It's the PI: "Quick coffee?"

LIEUTENANT
Go. Be nice to him. Let him talk. He trusts you. That's valuable.

FEMME FATALE
I feel like I'm feeding him to the wolves. He's already half crazy.

LIEUTENANT
(leaning in, voice low)
Sometimes you have to let the wolves fight each other. The mob took his partner. We just gave him the address. Let him do what he's going to do. When the dust settles, we'll be the ones standing on top of the pile.

Femme Fatale types a reply and stands. The Lieutenant watches her go. The warm smile disappears the second she's out of sight. The Lieutenant takes out a burner phone.

LIEUTENANT
(into phone, cold)
She just left to meet him. Keep eyes on both of them. When this is done, clean her up too.

She hangs up and sits alone in the darkening park - calm, in control.

[Act II continues with PI hotel room spiral, mob meeting, Lieutenant domestic kitchen scene, Femme Fatale killing the other girl in panic, Lieutenant killing Femme Fatale personally and staging the body in the PI's car as a "mob warning," PI discovering the body and believing the mob is coming for him, deciding to go to the country club on what he thinks is a justified mission.]

END OF ACT II

ACT III - SUICIDE MISSION + BITTER TWIST
Expanded Cinematic Draft - ~25-28 minutes

EXT. COUNTRY CLUB - NIGHT (FUNDRAISER)

The PI's car pulls up slowly to the large, well-lit country club. Music and laughter spill out from inside. He is visibly drunk, eyes glassy, one magazine in his gun. He knows this is a suicide mission. He believes the mob found out about Femme Fatale and killed her because of him. He steels himself, gets out, and walks toward the side entrance.

A MOB GUY in a cheap suit spots him and approaches.

MOB GUY
Hey, you can't park there--

The PI shoots him point-blank without breaking stride. The man drops. The PI keeps walking, gun up.

INT. COUNTRY CLUB - MAIN HALL - CONTINUOUS

Chaos. People scream and scatter. The PI moves through the room firing. He is not precise -- he is angry, drunk, and grieving. He burns through his magazine fast. When it clicks empty he drops the gun, grabs a heavy silver candlestick from a table, and starts swinging. He smashes one man across the face, takes his gun, and keeps moving deeper into the building.

Security tries to stop him. He shoots two more, takes hits himself -- a graze to the shoulder, a punch that splits his lip. He fights ugly. No style. Just forward momentum.

INT. HALLWAY - CONTINUOUS

The PI kicks open doors, looking for the boss. He is bleeding now, breathing hard. He finds another gun on a fallen man and keeps going.

INT. FEMALE MOB BOSS'S OFFICE - NIGHT

The FEMALE MOB BOSS (Ukrainian Jewish, 50s, sharp, inherited the organization after her husband's death) sits behind a large desk with her two SONS standing nearby. They are mid-discussion when the door bursts open.

The PI storms in, gun raised. The sons draw. Shooting erupts. The PI takes one son in the chest. The other son wounds the PI in the side before the PI drops him. The Female Mob Boss pulls a small pistol from her desk drawer.

They fire at the same time.

She is hit in the chest and dies almost instantly, slumping back in her chair. The PI is hit in the stomach. He staggers, drops his gun, and falls to his knees. Blood spreads across his shirt. He looks around the room -- the two dead sons, the dead woman who ran the organization. No satisfaction. Just emptiness.

He crawls to the large window behind the desk, pulls himself up, and sits with his back against the wall. He takes out a crumpled pack of cigarettes with shaking hands, lights one with difficulty, and exhales slowly. Through the window he can see the sunset fading over the golf course. He watches it in silence, cigarette burning down between his fingers. His breathing slows. His eyes stay open but the life goes out of them.

He dies sitting there, looking at the sunset.

EXT. COUNTRY CLUB PARKING LOT - NIGHT

The LIEUTENANT sits in her car across the lot, watching. She has been waiting. When the shooting stops and it goes quiet, she calls it in on her radio.

LIEUTENANT
Shots fired at the country club. Multiple victims. I'm on scene.

She gets out, walks in calmly, badge out. She is the first "official" on the scene. She moves through the bodies in the main hall, then finds the office. She sees the PI dead by the window, the Female Mob Boss and her sons dead. She takes a long look at the PI's body, then at the sunset through the window. No expression.

She steps back out before backup arrives.

INT. PRESS CONFERENCE - DAY

The Lieutenant stands at a podium in front of reporters and cameras. She is composed, professional.

LIEUTENANT
Last night, an undercover officer working to dismantle organized crime in this city gave his life in the line of duty. His actions led directly to the elimination of the leadership of one of the most dangerous criminal organizations operating in our region. We are still piecing together the full scope of his work, but what is clear is that he saved lives by ending a threat that had gone unchecked for too long.

She pauses, looks at the cameras.

LIEUTENANT (CONT'D)
He was a hero. And we will not forget what he did for this city.

INT. LIEUTENANT'S CAR - NIGHT (LATER)

The Lieutenant sits alone in her car after the press conference. She exhales, leans her head back against the seat. For the first time in a long time, she allows herself a small, private smile. She believes she has won. Everyone who could expose her is dead. The story is clean. She is safe.

She starts the car.

EXT. STREET - NIGHT

The same dark sedan from the Act I CI package drop pulls up slowly alongside her car. The driver -- we never see his face clearly -- raises a .38 revolver and fires three times through the open window. The Lieutenant is hit in the chest and side. She slumps over the wheel.

The driver tosses the gun into her car, then drives away into the night.

Music plays...

CREDITS.

THE END

No one wins.
The past always comes back.
