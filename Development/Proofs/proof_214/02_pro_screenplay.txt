THE KEYS

Developed draft — STUDIO Development / Story Department
Genre: Drama · Format: Short scene (~1 pg) · Two-hander, single location


FADE IN:

INT. ROBERT'S KITCHEN - LATE AFTERNOON

A tidy kitchen kept by someone with time on his hands. One coffee cup
in the rack. ROBERT, 78, sits at the table reading a folded newspaper
he isn't reading.

A car door outside. He turns the paper face-down.

KAREN, 45, lets herself in. Nurse's clogs, a casserole dish she sets
on the counter without being asked. She's done this drive a hundred
times. Today she doesn't take off her coat.

                    ROBERT
          You didn't have to bring food.

                    KAREN
          It freezes.

She sees it before he can hide it -- the dented fender through the
window, the mailbox splinted with duct tape. Neither mentions it.

Karen sets her car keys on the table. Her keys. A small, deliberate
sound.

                    ROBERT
          How are the boys.

                    KAREN
          They miss their grandpa.
              (beat)
          Dad. Dr. Ellison called me.

Robert smooths the newspaper. Smooths it again.

                    ROBERT
          Forty-one years I drove to that
          plant. Never put a scratch on
          anybody.

                    KAREN
          I know.

                    ROBERT
          Your mother and I took the coast
          road in that car. Before you.

                    KAREN
          I know, Dad.

She doesn't hold out her hand. She just waits. The casserole ticks as
it settles. Outside, a school bus hisses to a stop and pulls away.

Robert reaches into his cardigan pocket. Sets his keys on the table,
next to hers. Two sets. He doesn't let go right away.

                    ROBERT
          Sundays. You take me to the
          cemetery Sundays.

                    KAREN
          Every Sunday.

He lifts his hand. Leaves the keys.

Karen pockets them -- quietly, no triumph -- and finally takes off her
coat. She opens the fridge to make room for the dish. Stays a while.

                                              FADE OUT.

THE END
